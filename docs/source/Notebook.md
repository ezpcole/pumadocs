# Notebook & Interviews

## Introduction

### What is the Engineering Notebook?

* The Engineering Notebook is your team's method of displaying your design process. 
* This includes match strategy, iterations, programming, and the Engineering Design Process.

### What is the Engineering Design Process?

* The Engineering Design Process is the design process that teams use when making a robot. It consists of the five categories (see the [rubric](https://recf.org/documents/2024/11/notebook-and-interview-rubrics-vex-factory-automation-competition.pdf/) for more detail):
  * **Identify** -- short for "Identify The Problem", this section of the engineering notebook is about stating every "problem" and challenge of the game that you need to solve. This inclues game element analysis, defining strategy problems, problems with the robot (and for rebuilds), and rule updates. You should state your goals for your design in terms of how you address problems.
  * **Design** -- short for "Design, Prototype, and Brainstorm Solutions", this section of the engineering notebook covers the inital prototyping and designs you create to address the problems in the previous section. In this section, design solutions and ways you solve the challenges. **Using CAD for this will help a lot!** Some things you might want to include here are rough sketches of intakes, indexers, and outtakes, and potential drivetrain lengths, widths, rpms, and details. Anything prototyping should be in this section, including pros/cons charts of every prototyped design.
  * **Select** -- short for "Select the Best Solution and Plan", this section talks about the process of selecting the best design. The way you should document this is through the usage of design matrixes, but you should also note your plan to implement said solution. This includes things like where you're going to mount the selected subsystem.
  * **Build** -- short for "Build and Program the Solution", this section talks about the process of building and programming the solution. Here, you should talk about the different steps that you take in order to implement the given solution/subsystem. This section should have enough detail that any reader will be able to recreate your robot's design by reading this.
  * **Test** -- short for "Test Solution", this section talks about the ways you test the solution. It is important to establish the criteria that you're testing for and what is considered positive & negative outcomes, and the results of the tests.
  * **Iterate** -- short for "Repeat Design Process", this section should show that your design process is repeated in order to meet goals better and improve performance. You can show this through tournament analysis and results, and by showing your iteration number for the robot subsystem (or robot itself)
